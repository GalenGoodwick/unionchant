Organizations to contact for funding, testing, partnerships, and academic validation.

## Funding — Labor & Worker Power

### The Workers Lab
- **Website:** theworkerslab.com
- **What they do:** Accelerator and fund for innovations that build worker power
- **Why Union Chant fits:** This is their exact thesis — tools that give workers democratic voice. They fund early-stage projects.
- **Apply:** Check website for current cohort applications
- **Priority:** CALL FIRST

### AFL-CIO Technology Institute
- **Website:** aflcio.org
- **What they do:** The federation's technology and innovation arm, explores digital tools for the labor movement
- **Why Union Chant fits:** "47,000 members deliberated and chose this priority" is a stronger mandate than "our leadership decided." That pitch lands here.
- **Contact:** Look for their innovation or strategic initiatives team

### National Domestic Workers Alliance (NDWA)
- **Website:** domesticworkers.org
- **What they do:** Progressive, tech-forward labor organizing (built the Alia platform)
- **Why Union Chant fits:** Large distributed membership, no central workplace — exactly the kind of organization that needs digital deliberation
- **Contact:** Ai-jen Poo (Director) or their tech/innovation team

## Testing & First Participants

### Local Union Chapters (First Real Test)
- **Target unions:** SEIU, UNITE HERE, CWA, UAW, AFSCME
- **Approach:** Find one local with a tech-friendly organizer and a real upcoming decision (contract priorities, budget allocation, campaign strategy)
- **Pitch:** "Run your next member vote through Union Chant — free. See what happens when 500 members actually deliberate instead of just voting yes/no."
- **How to find contacts:** Labor councils in your city, union halls, labor organizer meetups
- **Priority:** THIS IS PHASE 1 — everything else follows from one real success

### DSA Chapters
- **Website:** dsausa.org
- **What they do:** Democratic Socialists of America — large membership, democratic internal structure
- **Why Union Chant fits:** They already struggle with internal decision-making at scale. Chapters vote on priorities, platforms, endorsements. Digital-native membership.
- **Approach:** Contact a large chapter (NYC, LA, Chicago, East Bay) and offer a pilot

### Participatory Budgeting Project
- **Website:** participatorybudgeting.org
- **What they do:** Run participatory budgeting in cities across the US and Canada
- **Why Union Chant fits:** They need exactly this tool — their current process is town halls + simple voting. Union Chant adds deliberation.
- **Contact:** Check website for partnership inquiries

### Sortition Foundation
- **Website:** sortitionfoundation.org
- **What they do:** Citizen assembly practitioners (random selection for deliberation)
- **Why Union Chant fits:** They understand deliberation deeply. Union Chant removes the scale ceiling on citizen assemblies (currently ~150 people max).

### newDemocracy Foundation
- **Website:** newdemocracy.com.au
- **What they do:** Australian organization running citizen juries and deliberative processes
- **Why Union Chant fits:** Same as Sortition Foundation — they'd grasp the algorithm immediately and could pilot it

## Academic Validation & Research Partners

### Stanford Deliberative Democracy Lab
- **Lead:** James Fishkin (coined "deliberative polling")
- **Website:** cdd.stanford.edu
- **Why:** THE leading academic authority on deliberation. A partnership or endorsement gives instant credibility. Fishkin has spent his career on exactly this problem.
- **Approach:** Email with a clear description of the algorithm and ask for feedback/collaboration

### MIT Center for Collective Intelligence
- **Lead:** Thomas Malone
- **Website:** cci.mit.edu
- **Why:** Studies how groups can be made collectively intelligent. The tiered tournament is a novel contribution to their research area.

### GovLab at NYU
- **Lead:** Beth Simone Noveck
- **Website:** thegovlab.org
- **Why:** Studies collective intelligence in governance. Runs CrowdLaw and other civic innovation programs. Active in connecting tech to government use.

### Computational Democracy Project
- **Website:** compdemocracy.org
- **What they do:** Built Pol.is (used by Taiwan's g0v/vTaiwan)
- **Why:** They understand the space deeply. Pol.is finds consensus in opinion data — Union Chant creates consensus through deliberation. Complementary, not competitive. Potential partnership.

## Suggested Order of Operations

1. **Incorporate the nonprofit** ($300-700)
2. **Contact The Workers Lab** — most aligned funder, also an accelerator
3. **Find one local union for a real pilot** — this is the proof point everything depends on
4. **Apply to Fast Forward** — accelerator for tech nonprofits
5. **Email Stanford Deliberative Democracy Lab** — academic credibility
6. **Apply to Democracy Fund and Knight Foundation** — larger grants
7. **Run the pilot, document results** — this becomes the case study for everything else
8. **Apply to remaining funders with real-world evidence**
